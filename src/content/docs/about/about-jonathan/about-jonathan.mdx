![Portrait of Jonathan Hussey](./portrait.jpg)

I'm a software architect and staff-level engineer with 14+ years of building systems that have to keep working: healthcare platforms where concurrent edits to patient data are a safety issue, analytics pipelines ingesting hundreds of millions of records a month, and AI-enabled platforms where the interesting problems start after the model call returns.

I started in web development and design, moved through energy and healthcare, then spent five years deep in cloud-native data engineering — the era that taught me most of what I believe about queues, failure, and the cost of not measuring things. These days I lead architecture while staying hands-on: designing the system, writing the load-bearing parts, reviewing everything else, and mentoring the team that will own it.

Two habits define how I work. I measure before and after — most of the numbers on this site exist because performance claims without baselines are just adjectives. And I treat failure paths as the actual design problem: durable queues, audit trails, replayable pipelines, and alerting that tells you what broke while it still matters.
