## Context

Cambridge past papers already contain a large body of revision material. However, the questions and marking schemes are distributed across separate PDFs, arranged by examination series rather than by the topic a learner needs to practise.

The IGCSE Past Paper Toolkit reorganises that material. A consolidation run accepts paired question papers and marking schemes, selects relevant questions, attaches their answers, removes reused items, and writes a focused set of outputs.

The source material remains authoritative throughout the process. Stored runs cover five subjects, but that evidence is a demonstration rather than a guarantee that every Cambridge layout will work without adjustment.

## The Problem

The central difficulty was that a PDF preserves appearance without exposing the examination structure needed by the toolkit. Text and coordinates are available, but there is no dependable record of where a question ends, whether nearby artwork belongs to it, or which marking scheme region supplies its answer.

Paper formats also differ. Multiple-choice answers often appear as compact rows, while structured answers can occupy blocks across several pages. Questions may be repeated in another series under a different number, and figures can extend below the final option or prompt.

The problem was therefore not simply text extraction. The pipeline had to reconstruct a complete question-answer unit and retain enough evidence to explain every boundary and match.

## Constraints

- **Source Fidelity.** Each question must retain its stem, options, diagrams, and tables; a clean text match is insufficient if the visual crop loses information needed to answer it.
- **Layout Variability.** Question markers, option groups, figures, footers, and page breaks move between papers, so no single fixed rectangle can define a question.
- **Answer Formats.** Compact multiple-choice rows and multi-page structured answers require different extraction paths selected before consolidation begins.
- **Cross-Series Duplication.** Reused questions should appear once in the output while every additional source remains recorded.
- **Traceability.** Missing answers and incorrect boundaries must remain visible through source paths, coordinates, statuses, and answer references in the manifest.

## The Design

Question papers are discovered by filename and paired with marking schemes through the corresponding `_qp_` and `_ms_` names. Before question extraction begins, the marking schemes are inspected for compact answer rows and structured answer blocks. The resulting classification selects the appropriate per-page or cross-page path.

The consolidation pipeline then passes structured question records through boundary analysis, topic matching, de-duplication, answer lookup, and output generation. The same extracted-question records can also be indexed for retrieval, but this remains a secondary path built on the extraction work.

Boundary analysis begins with numbers positioned in the expected question margin. Text evidence and sequence checks reject unrelated numbers, while the next accepted marker provides an initial lower boundary. For multiple-choice material, option detection and pixel inspection extend the crop when a diagram or wrapped option continues below the expected text region. Structured questions use page-spanning regions when the next boundary falls on a later page.

Topic matching compares extracted text with the requested term and its aliases. Additional filters can require, permit, or exclude terms, and a positional selector can choose specific question numbers. De-duplication then removes the displayed question number and page noise before comparing canonical text; multiple-choice questions receive a second signature based on meaningful stem and option tokens.

Answer lookup resolves each retained question against its paired marking scheme. Multiple-choice results use the compact option-and-mark rows, while structured results preserve the relevant answer text and page clips. Output generation writes a searchable questions PDF, an answers PDF, and a 31-field manifest CSV containing the source, answer, extraction coordinates, status, fingerprint, and duplicate history. A combined PDF is optional.

Similar-question search operates over a separate index built from the extracted records. A question answered incorrectly can be selected by supplying its paper filename and question number; that question becomes the source for a ranked set of related practice drawn from the rest of the index. Stored subject indexes demonstrate this workflow across 42 to 50 papers. Phrase search provides a second route for direct text matches, while hybrid search combines embedding similarity with keyword and token overlap and applies small syllabus and representation adjustments. Sentence Transformers provides the normal embedding path; if it is unavailable, deterministic NumPy token-hashing vectors allow the index to remain usable with reduced semantic quality.

The result is a pipeline whose important decisions remain inspectable. Stored outputs demonstrate its use across five subjects and both principal answer structures, while unfamiliar layouts remain an explicit detector-tuning boundary rather than an unqualified compatibility claim.
